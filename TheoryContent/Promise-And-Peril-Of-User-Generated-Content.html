Promise And Peril Of User Generated Content

Friday, 21 February

Web 2.0, also referred to as the social web, saw an increase in web user prosumption. The term prosumption is to define an individual who both produces and consumes on a particular platform. During this phase of web history, users of digital technology were able to interact and collaborate with each other through social media as creators, resulting in user-generated content.

User-generated content includes pictures, videos, text, audio and any other form of media that has been posted online by a user. The continued advancements in digital technology have allowed for increased accessibility and distribution, thus allowing for an easy platform to create and dispense user-generated content (hereafter referred to as UGC).

UGC is shared for various purposes, such as entertainment, news, marketing, research, awareness, and problem-solving. A continuous growth of individuals are taking advantage of the interactive opportunities in creating independent content, thus resulting in the democratization of content creation.

As digital technologies allow for speed in accessing the masses, there are many opportunities in the continued distribution and recirculation of UGC that present themselves. These opportunities include:

  • Users being able to bring awareness to issues within their communities and countries, such as #AmINext, #BlackLivesMatter and #FeesMustFall, which could bring about positive change on a large scale.
  • Users are able to provide key eyewitness content and information that may otherwise have been inaccessible
  • Access to reviews. With the continued use of e-commerce and online business dealings, users are able to access online reviews from other individuals to better understand a brand or product that they can no longer physically check before engaging with or purchasing it.
  • Users are able to share knowledge and form collective intelligence. Social groups such as fandoms have allowed for many to feel as though they are a part of a community especially in what can often be a divisive and disconnected world. This also allows users to gain perspectives from individuals that they wouldn't otherwise be able to engage with.
  • UGC also allows for easy marketing and reach for companies.

Although there being many benefits in UGC there are various disadvantages that present themselves. Such as;

  • Issues concerning privacy. In South Africa, the government introduced an act, Protection of Personal Information Act of 2013 (which I will review in the Review On Protection Of Personal Information Act blog post.) as an attempt to deal with this concern.
  • Continued breach of copyright law, as UGC is often based on copyrighted material.
  • Vigilantism without firm evidence.
  • Easy spread of fake news and false information.
  • Social media shamming and social discriminations.

User-generated content brings various opportunities to the digital society, regardless, there are many critical disadvantages that must be considered.
