Why I chose Google Street View

Posted Monday, 29 June

Andile Nkosi is a blogger, foodie, and aspiring influencer situated in Johannesburg. He loves to travel around Johannesburg finding new food to try and explore with. Furthermore, he loves sharing what he finds with his readers, so they too can visit these areas and experience them too. Having google street view embedded into the blog post for the top 5 restaurants allows readers to feel as if they are in the area physically. It gives them a realistic view of how the restaurants look like and the atmosphere around each place.

Google Street View is a compilation of panoramic photos that can be uploaded by businesses or customers. According to Anne Karasarides (2020), customer interest in business and engagement is likely to double if there is an inclusion of photos and virtual tours. Therefore, as an aspiring influencer Andile is able to encourage his website visitors to visit the actual restaurant represented in the post.

Due to the coronavirus pandemic, individuals were restricted in going to restaurants. As a result, to allow readers to still feel like they are connected to the real world, it was vital that the website provide reflects and challenges the surrounding that the readers cannot currently experience physically. Furthermore, as many of the restaurants are in locations that aren’t easily visible if it is the first time that an individual visits the area, having the street view Google map embedded in the site allows for users to have an idea as to what to look for when they do finally visit the area. Through this, the street view has allowed for the physical and the digital world to combine.

Besides creating a connection with the targeted audience, Google street view demonstrates quality in the website, as it reflects that the character cares about providing the best experience to the readers. Virtual viewing and tours are becoming increasingly ubiquitous. Over the years there has been an increase in platforms that provide similar experiences as to Google street.

Google Earth provides a satellite view imagery of Earth. HERE WeGo allows users to explore their surrounding world in 3D. Apple Maps allows for interactive 3D views, Flyover and turn-by-turn spoken directions. Other alternatives also include, but are not limited to, Mapillary, Modest Maps, Yandex.Maps, Bing Maps, and MapQuest. MapQuest has gone on to be one of Google maps’ top competitors in being the number one map site.

Regardless of the many alternatives that present themselves, I chose to use Google street view based mainly on how easy it is to access and familiarity. Many subscribers are likely to understand google maps (street view). As it often accompanies many android phones, moreover, it is part of Google which is currently dominating the market share (Oberlo, 2020).

Furthermore, a recent report (McGee, 2008:1) states that Google maps is ranked number one in terms of features and usability. To this end, readers and site visitors are likely to have increase ease in using Google street view based on familiarity

Having the Google street view map embedded into the website establishes a professional, sophisticated, and user-friendly experience to readers, which ties in with what the character aims to achieve on his personal website. It also allows users to feel as though they have, furthermore, been invited into his world.
