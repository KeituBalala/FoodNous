Important Moment In Web History

Posted On The Friday

In 1993, the Mosaic web browser was developed by students at the National Center for Supercomputing Applications (NCSA) at the University of Illinois. Of all developing web browsers at the time Mosaic had a competitive advantage based on the features it contained. At this point, the popularity of the Mosaic web browser had peaked.

However, due to conflicts with the University of Illinois the students, one of whom was Marc Andreessen, had to begin a new venture named Netscape. They then launched the Netscape navigator 0.9, a web browser. In a short period, the Netscape navigator was able to defeat the mosaic browser in terms of usage share of browsers.

It was not long after this that Bill Gates saw an opportunity in the development of web browsers. Microsoft was already popular at the time, however, it did not come with a browser meaning that on Microsoft Windows, you needed to buy(install) a web browser separately. It was then that Microsoft introduced Internet Explorer version one. Soon after the launch of version one Internet Explorer version 2, with major upgrades, was launched and challenged Netscape navigator. Over the years the companies began to rapidly compete in the upgrading of their browsers, a period that would be known as "The browser wars".

The browser wars saw the decline of Netscape. Netscape then open-sourced their code. This led to Mozilla Foundation creating a successor to their browser known as Mozilla Firefox. Many other browsers followed, such as The Opera web browser and eventually Google Chrome web browser.

Although not having arisen in the best ways, I believe that the competition midst 'the browser wars' saw a rapid increase in the improvement of the quality and efficiency of browsers. This allowing us to enjoy the internet in the ways we do today. Had there been no competition and only monopoly, the development of browsers would have been slower and less efficient as compared to now. For this reason, I choose ‘The browser wars’ as one of the most significant moments in web history.
